What Is Wrist Surgery?

Wrist surgery is performed to treat conditions that cause persistent wrist pain, instability, stiffness, or reduced hand function. The aim of wrist surgery is to relieve pain, improve wrist movement and restore strength and coordination of the hand and wrist.

Depending on the condition, wrist surgery may involve repairing ligaments, relieving nerve pressure, correcting joint alignment or addressing joint damage. Not all wrist problems require surgery-careful evaluation is always the first step.

Who Is This Surgery For?

Wrist surgery may be considered for patients who:

  • Have long-standing wrist pain not improving with medication or physiotherapy
  • Experience restricted wrist or hand movement affecting daily activities
  • Have wrist joint instability, ligament injuries or repeated strain injuries
  • Show structural wrist damage on X-rays, MRI or other imaging
  • Require stable wrist function for work, sports or lifestyle demands
